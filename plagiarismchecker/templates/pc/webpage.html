What is Plagiarism?

Plagiarism is the act of presenting someone else's work or ideas as your own, with or without their consent, by incorporating it into your work without full acknowledgement.

It's a serious academic and ethical offense that can lead to severe consequences like failing grades, suspension, or legal issues. Understanding and avoiding plagiarism is crucial for maintaining academic integrity.

Types of Plagiarism

  • Direct Plagiarism: Copying text word-for-word without credit.
  • Self-Plagiarism: Reusing your own old work without citing.
  • Mosaic Plagiarism: Changing a few words but keeping the original structure.
  • Accidental Plagiarism: Forgetting to cite sources unintentionally.
  • Source-Based Plagiarism: Misrepresenting or fabricating sources.

Consequences of Plagiarism

Academic Consequences

  • Failing grades or course failure
  • Academic probation or suspension
  • Degree revocation
  • Loss of scholarships or funding

Professional Consequences

  • Job termination
  • Loss of professional credibility
  • Legal lawsuits and fines
  • Damage to reputation
