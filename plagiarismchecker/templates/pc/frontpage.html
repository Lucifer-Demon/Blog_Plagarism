Types of Plagiarism

  • Direct Plagiarism: Copying word-for-word without credit.
  • Self-Plagiarism: Reusing your own old work without citing.
  • Mosaic Plagiarism: Changing a few words but keeping the original structure.
  • Accidental Plagiarism: Forgetting to cite sources unintentionally.
  • Source-Based Plagiarism: Misrepresenting or fabricating sources.

Consequences of Plagiarism

Plagiarism can result in academic penalties, loss of credibility, job termination, or even lawsuits. Always ensure your work is properly cited and original.

Understanding and avoiding plagiarism helps uphold integrity and trust in your academic or professional journey.
